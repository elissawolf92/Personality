Personality Assessment for Motion Styles in Character Animation

 

Purpose

The study aims to find a mapping between Laban Movement Analysis (LMA) parameters of a virtual human character and personality traits of the plasticity-stability personality model.   LMA  is a motion analysis technique that formally describes human  movement. Plasticity is the tendency to explore and deal novelty with flexibility and is an indicator of extroversion and openness. Stability describes an individual's tendency to maintain stability and avoid disruption in emotional, motivational and social domains.  You will compare two animated human characters performing the same action with different motion characteristics on a web-based application. The ultimate goal of this work is to apply our results to the simulation of heterogeneity in crowds by modifying the motion of human characters according to personality. Should you choose to participate, your answers will be uploaded to the Penn CGI Server and then stored in the researchers’ computers. Your total time is not expected to exceed 1 hour. You can perform the study on your own computer. This research is sponsored by the Department of Defense and is subject to review by the Army Human Research Protections Office.
